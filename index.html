What I learned in my freshman year as a Computer Science student:

In my freshman year I learned a lot of CS fundamentals (also a lot of Math). Specifically:

In my 1st semester I took C, Calculus I, Physics, Digital Design and English I.


In English I we analyse papers that were related to Computer Science fundamentals and we learned related vocabulary.


In C Programming Language, we did a lot of exercises such as Hangman, TicTacToe, how to handle files and we also Memory Managment.

Digital Design has an introduction to Computer Hardware, and there we learned Computer harware fundamentals such as Assembly language, Processor implementation, Pipelining, Memory System, Peripheral, RISC-V.

In Calculus I we saw integrals and functions.

Physics was all about mechanics, oscillation & mechanical waves and electromagnetism.


In my 2nd semester I had C++, Linear Algebra, Calculus II, Discrete Maths and English II.


In C++ we learned about classes, objects and Object Oriented Programming in general, Inheritance and Recursion.


Calculus II was the continue course of Calculus I.


Discrete Maths: You can find more information here: http://users.ics.forth.gr/~argyros/cs118.html


English II was the same thing as we did in English I.
